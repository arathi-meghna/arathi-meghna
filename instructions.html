Waste Management Instructions

Proper waste management is essential to maintain a clean environment and protect our planet. Below are some key guidelines:

1. Segregate Waste

Separate your waste into categories:

  • Biodegradable Waste: Food scraps, garden waste.
  • Recyclable Waste: Paper, plastics, glass, and metals.
  • Non-Recyclable Waste: Items like dirty tissues, diapers, or broken glass.

2. Reduce Waste

Be mindful of your consumption and try to minimize the waste you produce. Here are some ways to reduce:

  • Buy products with minimal packaging.
  • Use reusable containers and bags.
  • Compost organic waste when possible.

3. Recycle Properly

Ensure recyclables are clean and dry before placing them in recycling bins.

  • Rinse containers and bottles.
  • Avoid mixing recyclables with non-recyclables.
  • Check your local recycling program for specific guidelines.

4. Dispose of Hazardous Waste Carefully

Certain waste materials require special handling. These include:

  • Electronics (e-waste).
  • Used batteries.
  • Paints and chemicals.

Contact your local waste management facility for disposal instructions.

5. Educate Others

Help spread the word about waste management. Educate your family, friends, and community on proper waste disposal practices.
